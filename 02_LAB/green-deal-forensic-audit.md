# LAB 02 | Pillar 06: Green-Deal Forensic Audit (GDFA)
**Objective:** Certifying SME compliance with EU Green Deal Taxonomy for the COP31 Antalya Corridor.

## 1. THE TSI LEGACY
The GDFA methodology is rooted in the **Territorial Strategies for Innovation (TSI)** approach (10th EDF). As documented in *Toffanin et al. (2021)*, sustainable innovation requires a "Bottom-Up" territorial alignment paired with "Top-Down" institutional mandates.

## 2. THE MEDITERRANEAN TRIAD PROTOCOL
For SMEs operating in the **Bari-Ankara-Brussels** corridor, the audit focuses on:
* **Resource Sovereignty:** Aligning Turkish manufacturing (METU/Aselsan) with Italian Small Business agility (Confindustria Bari-BAT).
* **Circular Provenance:** Tracking the lifecycle of "Green Tech" to meet EU "Digital Product Passport" requirements.
* **Institutional Signalling:** Translating local environmental impact into the "Strategic Autonomy" language required by the EU Commission.

## 3. COP31 SPECIAL PROTOCOL (Antalya 2026)
In collaboration with **Anadolu Ajansı** and **Allied4Future**, this audit layer prepares companies for:
1. **The Green GTM:** AI SEO optimisation for "Taxonomy-Aligned" technologies.
2. **Forensic Transparency:** Version-controlled documentation of carbon-reduction claims.
3. **Joint-Venture De-risking:** Ensuring Italian-Turkish partnerships meet 2030 Agenda standards.
